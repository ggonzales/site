Software Quality Metrics
The Agile Assurance Group applies software quality metrics following industry best practices. This is where Agile engineers apply a scientific approach using a schedule of measurements to analyze and quantify software or the software’s specifications.
Creating a high-quality product by attacking defects and judging sustainability are achieved by evaluating correctness, maintainability, integrity and usability – all critical to increasing product lifespan via ultimate user satisfaction.

Applying software quality metrics is the gold standard in software development and guides all of our quality assurance and management.

Software quality metrics are classed into three categories: product quality metrics, process quality metrics and project quality metrics.

Agile views the essence of applying software quality metrics is investigating the relationships among in-process metrics, project characteristics and end-product quality. Based on Agile’s scientific evaluation, we engineer improvements in both process and product, viewing quality from the entire software life cycle including measuring the quality of maintenance and after support. The basics of software quality metrics are:

Product metrics.
Agile first describes the characteristics of the product such as size, complexity, design features, performance and quality. In product quality metrics, mean time to failure (MTTF), defect density, customer problems and customer satisfaction are the parameters the software is sifted through. Defect density and MTTF are the two main metrics for intrinsic product quality. At this stage of evaluation numerous attributes are investigated, including data communication, distributed functions, performance, heavily used configurations, transaction rate, online data entry, end-user efficiency, online update, complex processing, reusability, installation ease, operational ease and rapid facilitation of change.

Process metrics.
Here the focus is on how to improve software development and maintenance, from determining the effectiveness of defect removal during development, judging the response time of the fix process and establishing the pattern of testing. Phase-base defect removal patterns, defect removal effectiveness, defect density during formal machine testing and defect arrival patterns during formal machine testing are all part of process metrics.

Project metrics.
Getting a handle on the human and business aspect of the project requires an evaluation of all characteristics and implementations including, the number of developers, quality assurance and testers on the team, all costs, deadlines and scheduling and other mechanics throughout the software development life cycle.
Programs contain errors; the foundation of software evaluation is to identify risks, defects, failures and deliver a product as free from dysfunction as possible. Software quality metrics are a series of complicated processes, but the core elements – correctness, maintainability, integrity and sustainability – are generally applied across the board.

Correctness
Software has to work to be marketed and user satisfaction is the judge of the product’s lifespan – the proof is correct function judged by performance. The most common measure is the number defects per thousand (kilo or K) lines of code (LOC), referring to source code as opposed to machine code or assembly language. KLOC is the widely used measure of a program’s size and offers a measure of defects compared to lines of code.

Maintainability
All programs contain errors. Maintainability judges how quickly fixes can be implemented. Measuring maintainability is elusive; it’s not a perfect qualitative or quantitative tangent. But an indirect means of measuring maintainability is mean time to change (MTTC), or the time needed to implement a fix for defects. MTTC estimates the average time from error detection to correcting function, or the time it takes to review the change, design a modification, implement the fix and test the effectiveness of the repair.

Integrity
Achieving software security is complex. Software quality metrics judge a program’s vulnerability to attack. Integrity is measured by two parameters – threat (the probability of attack over product life span) and security (the probability that a predictable attack can be countered over time.) Integrity is the sum of threat multiplied by security.

Usability
Working products sell; dysfunctional products die before marketing or fail in the marketplace. Agile’s software quality metrics ensure the reduction in defects, maintainability, sustainability, integrity and security, and ultimate success is judged by user satisfaction. Usability is determined by the physical or intellectual skills users need to learn the system, the time it takes a user to be moderately efficient in operating the system and the level of increased productivity created by the product. Agile’s experienced staff devises precise user reviews and questionnaires to glean the ultimate determination of your product’s success – user satisfaction is always based on program functionality.

Applying software quality metrics is the overall paradigm guiding Agile’s quality assurance services, whether evaluating your product or developing our own for you – the key is the delivery of the highest quality software possible.
