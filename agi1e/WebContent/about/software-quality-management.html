The goal of our mature service delivery model is to ensure risk mitigation and failure prevention through sustainable and repeatable processes.

Software Quality Management
Agile Assurance Group’s Software Quality Management begins at the inception of your development project and hinges on detailed, consistent and clear communication between our team leaders and your engineers throughout the Software Development Life Cycle, including post-production analysis.
Using strategies under the auspices of the Agile Software Development methodologies, we oversee every aspect of quality, from test-driven development for incrementally functional components to continuous integration to usability and performance testing.

As practiced by the Agile Assurance Group, Software Quality Management is an iterative and holistic practice. Analysis, test planning, test design, actual testing (dynamic, static, manual and automated), defects reporting and tracking, quality analysis, measurements and defect prevention are interwoven into the entire development process under the guidance of our experienced managers.

Our Software Quality Management staff’s oversight and direction begins with detailed communication to fully grasp your product performance goals, testing performance to specified requirements and hinging on customer and stakeholder satisfaction. Our Software Quality Management Plan and practices ensure pragmatic project speed, production, time savings and cost control. The ultimate goal is iterative testing for the ultimate quality in incrementally deliverable software at ultimate speed at the lowest possible production cost.

Our Developers perform unit testing to check source code, ensure individual module interfaces function as specified, and guarantees the integrated system meets requirements. The ultimate goal is a functional, high-quality, deliverable product for your customers.

The benefits of Agile’s Software Quality Management Plan are vast when applied to your in-house developers, and include:

Productivity.
Developers spend more time coding and less time testing.

Quality.
Functionality and deliverability are known incrementally by all stakeholders; defect tracking and test statistics are precise and up-to-date for each sprint as supervised by Quality Management.

Less re-work.
Costs are reduced by implementing fixes and specification changes discovered earlier in the development lifecycle.

Sales.
Marketing and sales staff have full confidence in your end product.


The basics of Software Quality Management are easily specified but difficult to perform in-house by many of our clients. Agile ensures quality control throughout development; encourages a team-wide “Culture of Quality” among all stakeholders; shares responsibility of quality control throughout sprints and milestones; reduces project-specific learning curves and encourages continuity between team members to realize cost efficiencies. Fault avoidance and fault prevention become inherent in every aspect of development.

The basics of Quality Management seem intuitive yet differentiate from Quality Assurance, but the two are inherent under Agile Assurance Group’s guidance. Our Software Quality Management Plan and services focus on functionality, reliability, portability, compatibility, efficiency and maintainability.

Cost efficiencies are realized by constantly charging developers with realistic and shorter deadlines (Agile sprints) to optimize resources through the use of smaller and more efficient teams. Agile’s step-by-step management using the Agile Software Development methodologies adheres to industry standards and methods under CMMI, ISO, SEI and Agile SCRUM, resulting in minimum lead time, quicker product deliverability and the highest quality achievable per specifications.

Our Software Quality Management oversees Quality Assurance, Quality Planning and Quality Control. We guide developers through:

Standards and regulations
We follow industry best practices (SCRUM, ISO, CMMI and SEI) to produce, verify, evaluate and confirm work incrementally to ensure function and deliverability throughout the project lifecycle.

Quality and risk assessment.
Agile’s Quality Management team sets quality goals in sprints and milestones, establishing Quality Assurance specifications and standards specific to your components and systems, focusing on predictable problems through vigorous risk management.

Project specificity
Applying evolving new procedures, standards and tools to enhance Quality Assurance practices increases product lifespan and compatibility across multiple platforms.

Verification and evaluation.
Agile’s Quality Management leaders mentor developers on production of artifacts, well-defined engineering documentation that is efficient using project-specific standardized reporting templates as opposed to detail-laden end-project documentation. Managers perform in-process reviews to verify and evaluate and confirm artifacts.
