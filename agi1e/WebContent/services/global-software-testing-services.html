Global Software Testing Services
The Agile Assurance Group applies industry best practices following the Agile development methodology to provide a wide array of global software testing services.
Agile focuses on the individuals in each individual project; we take the team concept between project owners and Agile software testers seriously, more so than processes or tools. We strive for incrementally working software over comprehensive and often unusable documentation. It’s the hallmark of Agile’s staff and executive management, and our clients receive significant return on investment from it.

A variety of testing models fit our Agile development philosophy inherent in our global software testing services. Agile provides global software testing services covering all methods, choosing the method or combination of methods best suited to the project, from smoke to regression testing and encompassing installation, functionality, usability, look and feel, compatibility, performance, security and conformance. We provide all levels of testing, from basic unit to integration, system and acceptance.

Agile’s experienced testers – who have specific experience in development and programming – take our clients through design review, test planning, test designing, test environment set up, execution and test reporting. Using the Agile methodology, initiating the Software Testing Life Cycle often involves several components running simultaneously by team members.

The Agile Assurance Group’s priority is to satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery of high-quality potentially shippable software. We incorporate change even late in the testing process, providing our clients a competitive advantage through shorter testing periods. Our iterative practices center on daily communication between our engineers and our client’s business management, developers, quality assurance and planning staff – we work together throughout the entire process. Sustainability and increased product lifespan is ensured through our dedicated communication practices.

When providing our global software testing services, Agile doesn’t eschew traditional testing methods, but applies varying attacks based on priority and necessity. Agile Assurance Group doesn’t throw away the wheel; we just ride it with the Agile attitude. Our global software testing services are holistic, including:

Regression testing.
Agile’s global software testing services ensure changes, whether software enhancements or fixes, don’t adversely affect the product. Code changes affect functionalities. Regression testing ensures that one fix doesn’t create an error in another portion of software function. Agile performs impact analysis of all changes to identify areas of highest risk.

Smoke testing.
Our global software testing normally begins with smoke testing. This build verification testing ensures the most important functions in the software work and whether the build is stable enough to proceed with further testing. Major specified functions are validated and expose integration and other problems early in the development cycle for new software or enhanced software, whether performed manually or with tools and scripts. Smoke testing is not a substitute for functional or regression testing.

Exploratory testing.
Also known as simultaneous learning, test design or test execution, exploratory testing is best described as a style emphasizing the freedom and individual responsibility of the tester to optimize quality. Here learning, test design, execution and test implementation are mutually supportive, running parallel through the project test life cycle. The antithesis of exploratory testing is scripted testing, where test scenarios are designed in advance. In reality, both scripted and exploratory testing are used in Agile’s global software testing services, but exploratory testing leans toward the Agile methodology inherent in our name.

Black box testing.
Also knows as behavioral testing, this is where the internal, structural, overall designs and implementation of the software is unknown to the tester. “Black box” intuits that the tester is unaware of the software’s internal structure. Black box testing attempts to find incorrect or missing functions, interface issues, mistakes in data structures or external database access, behavioral or performance errors, and initialization and termination issues. The overall advantage to black box testing is that it is done from the user perspective, providing an unbiased review independent from developers.

White box testing.
Here Agile steps back from the user perspective and utilizes programming knowhow to test the core functionalities of the software. Our testers study the implementation code, determine all valid and invalid inputs and verify outputs against expected outcomes. White box testing can be commenced at early stages; we do not have to wait for the GUI to be available, and testing is thorough covering most risk paths. White box testing is extremely complex and is why Agile’s highly skilled testers have thorough knowledge of programming and implementation.

Gray box testing.
An obvious combination of white box and black box testing, where the internal structure is partially known and involves having access to internal data structures and algorithms for test design purposes but continues to test from the user, or black box, perspective.
