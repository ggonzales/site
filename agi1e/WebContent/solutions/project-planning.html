Project Planning
Software Project Planning is a critical aspect of Software Project Management – but at Agile Assurance Group,we believe it’s hardly the sole responsibility of the Project Manager.
Project Planning involves virtually all stakeholders and members of the development and support team and is a critical component of the Agile Software Development philosophy which defines all we do. Major components of our quality Software Project Planning includes:

  • Defining the software’s immediate and future architecture based on current and projected needs.
  • Creating Project Milestones based on Requirements Documentation and dependencies in the software’s architecture.
  • Creating a projected plan for sprints within milestones (not to be confused with the sprint planning that takes place during development and adapts to actual progress)
  • Creating tracks within sprints to determine the number of design and development resources each sprint can accommodate.
  • Creating a manpower plan to accommodate defined tracks.
  • Story writing workshops to have each developer define feature subsets from user perspectives and estimate required effort – which becomes the basis for estimating timelines and budgets. This is also an invaluable tool to use in estimating financial and timeline impacts of feature changes once development is underway.
  • QA Function: Audit to ensure stories match Business, User, and Functional Requirement Documents.
  • QA Function: Risk Assessments based on preliminary sprint planning and user stories.
  • QA Function: Test planning based on risk assessments and development of associated test cases.
  • QA Function: Further manpower allocation based on testing requirements.
The Agile Assurance Group is expert in Software Development Planning and ensuring it integrates into the larger Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC). We perform all of the above duties when executing end-to-end SDLC services. We are often tasked with augmenting a client’s in-house or outsourced development team with our Quality Assurance capabilities As Quality Assurance in all parts of the SDLC is one of our core competencies – we will be comfortable working with you in this capacity if your needs are limited to these areas.

The most common stakeholders in mature Software Development Planning are:

  • Service Delivery Director – Ultimate responsibility for Project Management oversight.
  • Project Manger – Ultimate responsibility for project success and day to day management of project.
  • Project Owner – client in supervisory/approver role.
  • Business Analyst – ensures the Software Development Plan meets documentation and needs of all stakeholders.
  • Technical Lead – responsible for architecture, milestone, and sprint planning. The technical lead is usually the “Scrum Master” running the daily stand-up Agile meetings.
  • Developers – responsible for writing user stories for tracks they are assigned. Developers are required to take ownership of their estimates during this process.
  • Quality Assurance Team Lead – responsible for assessing key risk factors in the development plan as well as ensuring user stories meet project needs.
  • Testers – responsible for creating test cases based on the Quality Assurance Team Lead’s risk assessments. These same testers will later be responsible for executing the test cases they design.
The tasks and stakeholders described on this page are representative of our mature software service delivery model. There are great benefits derived from using the kind of diversified labor force represented in our cross-functional teams. Efficiencies are achieved that cannot be replicated by having a team of a few developers that attempt to fulfil all of these duties. This is one of the most common reason clients outsource to the Agile Assurance Group – our company achieves economies of scale that allow us to keep specialized labor forces that are simply out of reach of most medium size companies.
