Development
Are all development services the same? We think not.
We all know a guy that works cheaper, can work on ten projects at once, and can start a new project tomorrow. That guy does not work for Agile Assurance Group. As our name implies, we are practitioners of the Agile Development Methodology. Some people mistakenly think that Agile means there is no need for planning or rigid processes. Some of the guiding principles you will find we use to execute development:

Planning.
Much to some of our client’s surprise, we do not write code on day one of a project; and we do not deliver final estimates without a final plan. Your investment in a proper project plan will save you time, money, and frustration. We do not work any other way.

Quality Obsessed.
All of our development projects are accompanied by a QA compliment. As a rule, we will not develop without them – and you will accept nothing less if you want a working deliverable on time.

Continuous Integration.
In Agile Development, each sprint is designed to end with a potentially shippable incremental product. Taken one step further, continuous integration means the working code base is updated and tested as often as possible – ideally on a daily basis.

Colocation.
We all work in the same office, during the same working hours. The work from home concept (in our experiences) is frustrating at best. Good development requires constant communication among all team members at all times work is being done. If you have had an experience with a project where staff are not communicating and not aligned you will understand.

Ownership.
Developers estimate their own work and take ownership of those estimates, period.

Client Involvement.
We encourage clients, of the referred to as the Project Owner, to participate in daily standup meetings. This is a critical component in reducing double work and making sure that the client gets what they want.

Management.
The very best developers in the world need to be managed. There is no exception to this rule.

Dedication.
This is one of the big ones. Projects cannot share developers. Our projects must be large enough to support at least one full time developer and one full time QA engineer. Developers are not superhuman, and developers cannot stay efficient while working on more than one project at once. Managing a developer on multiple projects is not a responsible use of our client’s money and it is not the kind of juggling we do.
